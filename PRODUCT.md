# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Technical readers, engineering peers, potential consulting clients, recruiters,
AI-assisted CV screening systems, and collaborators who arrive through search,
direct links, or project references. They are usually trying to understand Adel
Nizamutdinov's judgment through writing, shipped products, consulting focus, or
CV evidence.

## Product Purpose

This personal site exists to build technical writing credibility and support
SEO. It publishes clear software engineering arguments, presents selected work,
and gives visitors enough context to trust the author's practical engineering
judgment. Success means search visitors can find focused technical pages,
understand the position quickly, and move naturally to related writing,
consulting contact, products, GitHub, or CV. The CV must also be easy for
recruiters, ATS tools, and AI screening systems to parse as senior product
engineering experience, with concrete technical keywords attached to real
product work rather than stuffed into generic lists.

## Brand Personality

Direct, pragmatic, technically sharp. The voice should be specific, calm, and
unsentimental: it explains what matters, why it matters, and what to do next
without hype.

## Anti-references

No named anti-references. Avoid generic SaaS landing-page patterns, decorative
AI-generated editorial tropes, portfolio gloss, and visual flourishes that
compete with reading, search clarity, or technical credibility.

## Design Principles

- Reading is the product: keep prose scannable, dense where needed, and
  comfortable for long technical articles.
- Search clarity first: titles, descriptions, headings, and internal paths
  should describe the actual topic without keyword stuffing.
- CV machine clarity matters: preserve plain semantic structure, direct dates,
  recognizable role titles, product/company names, and concrete skills so
  recruiter tools can extract the career story accurately.
- Specificity earns trust: prefer concrete claims, named tools, code-shaped
  evidence, and explicit tradeoffs over broad positioning.
- Minimal chrome, strong hierarchy: navigation and styling should support
  content discovery without turning every page into a campaign surface.
- Multilingual parity matters: English, Russian, and Tatar surfaces should stay
  structurally aligned when pages have translated counterparts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, readable contrast, semantic
headings, useful link text, reduced-motion alternatives, and layouts that remain
readable on narrow screens and in print-oriented CV contexts.
